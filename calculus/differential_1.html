Differentiable functions

A given function can be differentiated only if:
  1. a tangent can be produced on the desired point of the function,
  2. the function doesn't "reflect" off of any potentially constructed line,
  3. the function doesn't jump.

Method

I) The difference

\[\Delta y = f(x + \Delta x) - f(x)\]

II) The difference quotient (or rise over run)

\[\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{f(x + \Delta x) - f(x)}{\Delta x}\]

III) The differential quotient

\[\lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{f(x + \Delta x) - f(x)}{\Delta x} \Rightarrow \frac{\mathrm df(x)}{\mathrm dx} \Rightarrow f'(x)\]

The product rule

\[f(x) = g(x) \cdot h(x)\] \[f'(x) =~?\]

I) The difference

\[\Delta y = g(x + \Delta x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x) - g(x) \cdot h(x)\]

II) The difference quotient (or rise over run)

\[\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{g(x + \Delta x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x) - g(x) \cdot h(x)}{\Delta x}\]

Add a complicated zero: \(g(x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x) - g(x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x)\)

\[\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{g(x + \Delta x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x) - g(x) \cdot h(x) + g(x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x) - g(x) \cdot h(x + \Delta x)}{\Delta x}\] \[\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{[g(x + \Delta x) - g(x)] \cdot h(x + \Delta x) + g(x) \cdot [h(x + \Delta x) - h(x)]}{\Delta x}\]

I) The difference

\[\lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{g(x + \Delta x) - g(x)}{\Delta x} \cdot \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} h(x + \Delta x) + \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} g(x) \cdot \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{h(x + \Delta x) - h(x)}{\Delta x}\] \[\frac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx} = \frac{\mathrm dg(x)}{\mathrm dx} \cdot h(x) + g(x) \cdot \frac{\mathrm dh(x)}{\mathrm dx}\]
